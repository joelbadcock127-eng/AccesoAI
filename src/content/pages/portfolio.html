02 Case Study No.1

AI-built software that runs an entire club.

Devonport Table Tennis Association · the club's operating software, live

Devonport Table Tennis Association is a volunteer-run club in North West Tasmania with a genuinely unique problem. Running a season means draws, results, ladders, player averages, memberships, and years of history, all tangled together in spreadsheets, printouts, and the heads of a couple of dedicated volunteers. Off-the-shelf products don't fit how a local pennant competition actually works, and custom software built the traditional way would cost far more than a small club could ever justify.

This is exactly the kind of problem AI changes. Acceso AI's founder Joel is also the club's treasurer, and he used AI to build the club its own operating software: he described how the club actually runs in plain language, and AI wrote, tested, and refined the code in short, iterative batches. Custom software shaped precisely around one club's unique needs, at a cost a volunteer committee could say yes to. No software team. No agency budget.

The result is not just a website. It is the operating software for the whole club, with everything handled in one place. The full season draw is published, results are entered once and the ladders and player averages update instantly, members log in to their own dashboard, and the committee runs the entire competition from a single admin panel.

Before

  • Season draw kept in spreadsheets and printouts
  • Results typed up and passed around by hand
  • Ladders and averages recalculated manually each week
  • Club knowledge held by a couple of volunteers

After

  • Fixtures, results, and ladders live on one website
  • Results entered once, everything updates instantly
  • Player averages and history available to everyone
  • Members log in, the committee runs it from one admin page
The Result One piece of AI-built software now runs the whole club.
